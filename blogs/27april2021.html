Let's try Lucid Dreaming

I’ve been a soldier, a street fighter, a billionaire, and a Mortal Kombat fighter, but all in my dreams. Whenever I wake each morning realizing that it was all a dream, I wish I had known it so that I could control myself. But it’s always like watching a movie.


Imagine being able to be aware in your dreams, to do whatever you want, to date whomever you desire, and to experience anything that's possible (or not possible?). Oooh, this sounds interesting, doesn’t it?

Well, I have found that it’s possible and how you can do it.


I’ve heard of lucid dreaming for a long time, but didn’t have the proper technique to try it. But recently I came across this blog post of Tim Ferriss and I think it’s time for me to try this once and for all.


For the next two months, I’ll be keeping a dream diary next to my bed in which I’ll note down whatever I remember just after opening my eyes (this will require a lot of discipline). Also, I’ll be doing reality checks every once in a while to train my brain, like checking the patterns in the wall or time in the clock.


If you find the topic of lucid dreaming fascinating, why not try it out for yourself? Read this blog for more information.
