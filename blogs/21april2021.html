All the way to the well and back

A parable worth sharing:


Once there was a spiritual guru. He lived in a hut deep down in a jungle with two of his disciples. One day he decided to test his disciples, so he called both of them in the middle of the night outside his hut.


He handed both of them a lantern and said, “If you walk straight down this path for about 30 minutes, you’ll come across a well. Whoever first brings me a glass of water would receive a reward.”


One of the disciples said, “It’s almost impossible. It’s so dark right now─I can’t even see the path. How are we supposed to go to the well with just a lantern?”


While he was complaining, the other disciple moved slowly towards the well, holding his lantern in his hand. After a while, he returned back with a glass of water from the well.


When the disciple who was complaining asked him how he was able to go through the dark without seeing the whole path, he answered, “Yes, I couldn’t see the whole path, but I could see what was in front of me. And by taking one step forward at a time, I was able to walk all the way to the well and back.”


The moral: You'll move forward once you start moving forward.
