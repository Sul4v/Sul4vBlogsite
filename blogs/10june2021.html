That lil girl

Humans are the only animals that are aware of their existence in this world and that death is inevitable. So being a human, it’s my innate nature to think about life and death, after-life and before-life.


In 1954, the first human organ was transplanted from one human to another, and death was cheated. By the late 60s, liver, heart, and pancreas was successfully transplanted.

The only body organ that humans are unable to transplant is the brain, because of its overly-complex system.


I found it quite interesting, so I asked myself, “What if humans somehow found a way to transplant the brain too?”


Let’s do a thought-experiment on John:

At first, let's cut off both of his hands and legs. He’d look miserable, but he’d still be alive.

Then we'd transplant his liver, both of his kidneys, pancreas, stomach, lungs, … , and finally the heart.

If done successfully, he’d still be alive — maybe in a bad shape, but alive.


But his body has completely changed. His organs are not his, and the only part that’s left of him is his brain.

What this means is John is nothing but his brain. Nothing other than the brain.


If we could somehow transplant his brain to someone else’s body, he basically changed his body! Say, if a 60 years old man transplants his brain to a 15 years old boy, he basically reduces his age by 45 years.

This is mind-boggling.


Some would say, “What about the soul? Does it also come with the brain?”

I don’t know.


This is just a theory, which obviously isn’t possible as of now. But if in the future, humans somehow cracks the code for brain-transplant, this is something that’s worth pondering on.
