She looked cute

A couple months ago, I was returning home by a bus. I was sitting beside a girl, who was about my age and looked cute too. In my head I was thinking, Okay, I have to talk to her! But what if she isn’t interested? I glanced at her. She noticed. Shit, it’s awkward now.


Come one Sulav, just start with a simple question. It’s now or never.


“Ummm, which school do you go to?” I asked her with the deepest voice I could make, faking my confidence.


“St. Xavier’s school. And you?” She asked.


Wait a minute! Is she interested in keeping up the conversation?


“Well, I graduated from United Academy, but haven’t joined a college yet.”


“Oh really? Me too actually. I’m doing an internship these days, and searching for colleges as well. Have you decided which college you’ll be going to?” She asked me again. Woh!


“I’ve applied to a few US colleges and I’m waiting for the decisions to come out.”


“That’s great! I hope they’ll accept you.”


At this point, I’m trying my best not to smile by faking a serious look on my face.


“What’s your facebook id? I want to congratulate you after you get accepted.”


No way. It’s not happening. Did she just ask me for my id? We haven’t even exchanged our names yet. Oooh!


“I have deactivated my Facebook account, but I can give you my instagram id instead,” I replied.


“That would be even better,” she blushed.


We talked for a while until I had to get off the bus. But she DMed me on instagram later that night.



And that was a profound experience my fellas! A fucking boost for my confidence!


She later revealed that she also wanted to talk to me before I had even started the conversation. This made me realize how many girls I could’ve talked to, only if I had built up the courage to start up a conversation.


The point is: You never know what’s on the other side of the fear.


Now for those of you who are wondering, “Is he really that shy around girls?”


Yes! 100% yes! Not just girls, but with boys too. The thing is, I’ve been an introverted boy since I was little. But, recently I’ve been working to fix that.
The above was a Comfort Challenge, designed to push my comfort zone.


What I’d ask you to do is, whenever you’re in public, try to talk to a person of opposite gender similar to your age, and ask for their number/id (Even if you don’t wanna be in a relationship with them, try this!). Worst case scenario? They’ll say no.


Even if you get rejected, who cares? There are lots of other fish in the sea baby!


I'll share you a secret. I've talked to like 14/15 girls since that first one. 0 rejections.
